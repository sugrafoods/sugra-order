Our Story

How Sugra Began

"You remember the smell before you remember the taste."
01
Aaji's Kitchen
Every morning, the same ritual. Aaji would stand at the chulha, slow-stirring ghee in a heavy brass vessel. The whole house would fill with that golden, nutty aroma. As kids, we didn't know what made it special — we just knew nothing else tasted like it.
02
Something Changed
Years passed. The brass vessel was replaced by store-bought jars. The ghee looked the same but something was off. The aroma was missing. The warmth was gone. Food didn't feel the same anymore. We had accepted convenience and lost something irreplaceable.
03
The Search
We went looking for ghee that tasted like memory. We visited farms, spoke to elders, learned the Bilona method. We found Gir cows grazing freely, their A2 milk churned by hand into curd, then slow-simmered on a wood fire. The smell came back.
04
Back to That Kitchen
Sugra was born to bring that kitchen back — not just for our family, but for yours. Every jar is crafted the way Aaji would have approved: no shortcuts, no preservatives, no compromise. Just pure ghee, made with patience and love.
